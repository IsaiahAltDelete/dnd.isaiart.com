Subclasses

Wizards choose their subclass at **Level 3**. Each subclass specializes in a different school of magic, offering unique abilities and playstyles. Below are the four subclasses included in the *2024 Player’s Handbook*:

1. Abjurer

School of Abjuration: Masters of protective magic, Abjurers excel at shielding themselves and their allies from harm. They are the ultimate defensive Wizards.

  • Arcane Ward (Level 2): Create a magical ward that absorbs damage. The ward’s HP equals twice your Wizard level + your Intelligence modifier. You can refill the ward’s HP using spell slots as a Bonus Action.
  • Projected Ward (Level 6): Extend your Arcane Ward to protect allies within 30 feet.
  • Spell Breaker (Level 10): *Counterspell* and *Dispel Magic* are always prepared. If you fail to cast *Counterspell* or *Dispel Magic*, the spell slot is not expended.
  • Improved Abjuration (Level 14): Add your Proficiency Bonus to the saving throws of your Abjuration spells.

Playstyle: Abjurers are tanky and supportive, perfect for parties that need protection. Use *Arcane Ward* to absorb damage and *Counterspell* to shut down enemy casters.

2. Diviner

School of Divination: Diviners are masters of foresight and manipulation. They use their abilities to control the flow of battle and outmaneuver their enemies.

  • Portent (Level 2): Roll two d20s at the start of each day. You can replace any attack roll, saving throw, or ability check made by you or another creature with one of these rolls.
  • The Third Eye (Level 6): Gain 120-foot Darkvision and the ability to cast *See Invisibility* without expending a spell slot.
  • Expert Divination (Level 10): When you cast a Divination spell of 2nd level or higher, regain a spell slot of a lower level.
  • Greater Portent (Level 14): Roll three d20s for your Portent feature instead of two.

Playstyle: Diviners are strategic and versatile. Use *Portent* to guarantee success on crucial rolls and *Expert Divination* to conserve spell slots.

3. Evoker

School of Evocation: Evokers are masters of destructive magic, specializing in dealing massive area-of-effect damage while protecting their allies.

  • Sculpt Spells (Level 2): Choose up to 1 + the spell’s level creatures to automatically succeed on saving throws against your Evocation spells and take no damage.
  • Potent Cantrip (Level 6): If a creature succeeds on a saving throw against your cantrip, it still takes half damage.
  • Empowered Evocation (Level 10): Add your Intelligence modifier to the damage of your Evocation spells.
  • Overchannel (Level 14): Maximize the damage of your Evocation spells, but take damage equal to the spell’s level each time you use this feature.

Playstyle: Evokers are the ultimate damage dealers. Use *Sculpt Spells* to safely unleash *Fireball* and *Overchannel* to maximize damage in critical moments.

4. Illusionist

School of Illusion: Illusionists are masters of deception, using their magic to confuse, distract, and manipulate their enemies.

  • Improved Minor Illusion (Level 2): Cast *Minor Illusion* as a Bonus Action, and it can now create both sound and image simultaneously.
  • Malleable Illusions (Level 6): Change the nature of your Illusion spells as an Action.
  • Phantasmal Creatures (Level 10): Gain *Summon Beast* and *Summon Fey* as always-prepared spells, usable once per Long Rest without expending spell slots.
  • Illusory Reality (Level 14): Make parts of your illusions real for 1 minute. For example, an illusory bridge can support weight.

Playstyle: Illusionists are creative and unpredictable. Use *Minor Illusion* to distract enemies and *Illusory Reality* to create tactical advantages.

Tips and Strategies

  • Ritual Casting: Use rituals like Detect Magic and Leomund’s Tiny Hut without preparing them to save spell slots.
  • Scholar Expertise: Focus on Arcana or History to decode ancient lore or negotiate with factions.
  • Abjurer Defense: Pair Arcane Ward with Shield to become a frontline tank.
  • Illusionist Creativity: Use Minor Illusion as a Bonus Action to distract enemies or create cover.

Conclusion

The 2025 Wizard is a versatile and powerful class, perfect for players who enjoy strategic spellcasting and creative problem-solving. With streamlined mechanics and updated subclasses, Wizards remain a cornerstone of any adventuring party. Use this guide to master the arcane arts and dominate your next campaign!
