The Species

An Overview of Sims

Sims are a humanoid species from a parallel material plane known as The Neighborhood — a reality operating under fundamentally different physical laws than Faerûn. In their world, needs are quantified, emotions are color-coded, and a glowing diamond floats above every living creature's head at all times.

A Note on Lifespan

Sims age in distinct life stages. In their home world an adult Sim lives approximately 24 in-world days — roughly 90 days total from infant to elder. Planar travel has disrupted this cycle. A Sim in Faerûn may age at standard Sim rates, at Faerûnian human rates, or at no rate at all. They do not know which. They are choosing not to ask.

Life Stages

The Seven Ages of a Sim

Unlike the long slow aging of elves, Sims progress through clean discrete stages. The transition is instantaneous and involves a brief light show nobody fully understands.

👶
Days 0–3

Infant

Immobile. Cannot speak. All Needs drain at triple speed. Cannot act in combat except to be carried.

🍼
Days 3–7

Toddler

Walks, climbs, falls. Learns one Skill permanently. Half speed. Advantage on Stealth from size.

🧒
Days 7–20

Child

Develops Personality traits. Cannot die of Old Age. −2 to all STR and CON saves. Can use simple weapons.

📚
Days 20–34

Teen

Moody by default. Plumbob cycles at 3× rate. +2 CHA but all emotional penalties doubled.

🧑
Days 34–58

Young Adult

Peak condition. All careers accessible. Full racial traits active. Optimal adventuring stage.

👔
Days 58–82

Adult

Established. +1 INT. Wisdom proficiency applies to one additional skill of choice.

👴
Days 82–90+

Elder

+2 INT and WIS. −2 STR and CON. Speed 25 ft. Daily Old Age Death Saving Throws begin.

☠ Elder Old Age Death Saving Throws

Starting on Day 82, the Elder Sim makes a CON saving throw each in-game day. The DC escalates as they push past their expected lifespan. Three consecutive failed saves trigger death regardless of current DC. The Grim Reaper arrives with a trumpet. A Sim may attempt their Death Bargain (CHA DC 20) as one final action.

The Plumbob

The Diamond Above

A translucent diamond-shaped gem floats approximately one foot above the head of every Sim at all times. It is not jewelry. It is not magical in any Faerûnian sense. It simply is.

The Plumbob shifts color to reflect the Sim's emotional state. It cannot be hidden, covered, dispelled, or removed. Detect Magic reveals it as conjuration of a school unknown to Faerûnian theory. True Sight reveals it as part of the Sim's fundamental essence. The Sim cannot perceive their own Plumbob. They are the only being who cannot see it.

Mood Sight — Racial Gift

The Sim automatically knows when someone is lying, aroused, frightened, or grieving. This is not Detect Thoughts. It is simply how Sims perceive other beings. They gain advantage on all Insight checks. They cannot turn it off.

The Motives

Six Fundamental Needs

Sims have six core Needs that must be periodically met. Track each on a 0–10 scale. Extended stress, imprisonment, or long journeys produce cascading mechanical consequences.

🍔HungerDepletes every Long Rest
3 rests unmet: Sad mood. 7 rests: Incapacitated — 1 Exhaustion. 10 rests: Death by Starvation. The Sim will vocally complain the entire time.
💬SocialEvery 24 hours
Unmet: Will attempt conversation with anything — enemies, animals, furniture. 3 days: Sad permanently. 5 days: Depressed. This is compulsion, not rudeness.
🎨FunEvery 24 hours
Unmet: Tense mood. What counts as "fun" is determined by Aspiration. The DM decides. Arguing counts for some of them.
💤EnergyStandard Long Rest
Unmet: Sleeps anywhere without warning. Falls asleep mid-conversation at Critical. Each level of deprivation adds 1 Exhaustion. They will nap during a negotiation.
🛁HygieneEvery 48 hours
Critical: Visible green miasma. Creatures within 10 ft. make CON DC 12 or are Poisoned (nauseated). The Sim is aware. They are embarrassed. They started six fights today.
🚽BladderVaries by intake
Critical: A distinctive animation plays visible to all observers. Movement halves. At absolute failure, a puddle appears. Produces Mortified Plumbob. Other party members make CON DC 10 to keep straight faces.
Emotional States

The Twelve Moods

The Plumbob reflects twelve documented emotional states. Emotions are triggered by Needs, interactions, Occult spells, and environmental Moodlets.

Happy

+1 to all ability checks. Default adventuring state.

Elated

Advantage on CHA checks. +5 ft. movement. Plumbob visibly glows. Talks too much.

Fine

No effect. Baseline neutral. Most NPCs live here and are bored about it.

Tense

Disadvantage on Concentration saves. Paces involuntarily. May snap at allies.

Angry

+2 attack rolls, −2 WIS saves. Red plumbob. Will pick fights.

Sad

Disadvantage on social checks. Sighs before speaking. Finds rain relatable.

Flirty

Advantage on Persuasion, Disadvantage on Intimidation. Winks at everything.

Depressed

−1 all checks. Cannot use Skill Grind. Stares at walls. Refuses snacks.

Mortified

Black Plumbob. CON save DC 13 or die of Embarrassment within the hour.

Playful

Advantage on Performance. Spells have 10% chance of goofy visual effect.

Focused

+2 to INT checks. Concentration saves at Advantage. Will not hear when spoken to.

Inspired

Attempt unproficient skills at +3. Inspired speech grants allies 1d4 Bardic Inspiration.

Sub-Races

Occult Variants

Just as elves have Drow, Wood, Sea, and High variants, Sims have multiple sub-species called Occults. These may be present at character creation or acquired through specific planar events, curses, bites, or eating the wrong fruit.

🧛Forgotten Hollow

Vampire Sim

Sired, not born. Immortal. Perpetually dramatic.

Sims converted by the Dark Form. Feed on plasma (blood), die in sunlight, develop a branching tree of Powers and Weaknesses. Ranks: Fledgling → Minor → Prime → Master → Grand Master.

Mechanics: +2 STR, +1 CHA, Sun Vulnerability (2d6 radiant/round), Plasma Drain bite (heals self), Mist Form (Grand Master), Immortality.
🔮Glimmerbrook

Spellcaster Sim

Trained in the Realm of Magic. Three schools, one overcharge problem.

Studied under Sages of Practical, Mischief, or Untamed magic. Cast spells using Spell Charges. Brew potions through Alchemy. Ranks: Neophyte → Apprentice → Acolyte → Adept → Master → Virtuoso.

Mechanics: +2 INT, cantrips from chosen school. Overcharge triggers Wild Magic Surge.
🧜Sulani

Mermaid Sim

Island-born, saltwater in their blood. Dangerous when singing.

Full aquatic transformation — legs become tail when submerged. Communicate with dolphins. Can summon storms and lure victims into the ocean.

Mechanics: +2 CHA, Swim 60 ft., water breathing, Siren's Lure 1/long rest, dehydrates after 24 hours from saltwater.
🐺Moonwood Mill

Werewolf Sim

Cursed by a scratch. Transformed by the moon.

Afflicted with lycanthropy. Transform under full moons or Fury. Bound by Pack loyalty. Ranks: Pup → Runt → Prime → Veteran → Apex.

Mechanics: +2 STR, +1 CON, Claws 1d6+STR, Fury (Angry Plumbob = risk transform), Pack Bond, full moon forced shift.
👻Any World

Ghost Sim

Died, came back, did not leave. The Plumbob is frozen.

Plumbob is fixed at the emotion experienced at death. Can pass through walls. Cannot be healed normally. Ranks: Wispling → Ethereal Novice → Phantasmal Ascendant → Spectral Luminary → Transcendent Wraith.

Mechanics: Resistance all damage except radiant/psychic. Pass through nonmagical barriers. No healing spells.
🧚Enchanted Areas

Fairy Sim

Ancient nature magic. Emotions as currency. Gnomes as minions.

Manipulate emotions, luck, relationships, and plant life through Emotional Force — a magical pool fed by strong feelings.

Mechanics: +2 WIS, +1 CHA, Emotional Force pool (max 10), Fly speed 30 ft., can animate gnomes and age up creatures.
👽Sixam

Alien Sim

From the world beyond the stars. Politely invasive.

Green-skinned Sims from planet Sixam. Telepathic. Disguise themselves as normal Sims. Prone to abducting others for documented scientific research.

Mechanics: +2 INT, Telepathy 30 ft., Disguise Self at will, Brain Powers, Vulnerability to mind magic.
🌱Greenhouse / Enchanted

PlantSim

The Gardener who ate the wrong fruit.

Transformed into living plant-humanoids via Forbidden Fruit. Green skin, leafy hair. Photosynthesize. Vulnerable to fire.

Mechanics: +2 WIS, Photosynthesis replaces Hunger, Water Need replaces Bladder, Vulnerable Fire, Druidic Speech with plants.
🤖Any Lab

Servo / PlumBot

Constructed, not born. Fully sentient. Has a Plumbob anyway.

Robotic Sims built through Robotics skill. Equip Enhancement Modules for specialized capabilities.

Mechanics: Construct type, no Hunger/Energy/Hygiene/Bladder needs. Needs Charge. Vulnerable Water. Immune poison, disease, charm, sleep.

☠ A Note on the Grim Reaper

The Grim Reaper is a CR 22 entity who does not fight, does not negotiate beyond the Death Bargain, and finds the whole experience tedious. He carries a list. He has never lost a scheduled appointment — though a sufficiently charismatic Sim has caused him to reschedule. Once. He still thinks about it.

Language

Simlish

Simlish is the native language of all Sims. A fully grammatical, complete language that no creature in Faerûn speaks. It sounds like Italian, Welsh, Korean, and Ukrainian had an argument inside a blender. Linguists have attempted study. None have published findings. One retired.

"Sul sul."
— Hello / Goodbye. All-purpose greeting. The Sim uses this for arrivals, departures, waking up, and occasionally combat.
"Dag dag."
— Farewell. More formal. Used at the end of extended partings or very satisfying meals.
"Zoop!"
— Exclamation of surprise. Most commonly heard in combat. Also used when something expensive breaks.
"Plerg."
— A mild insult. The Sim doesn't know why it bothers people. It bothers people enormously.
"Nooboo."
— Baby. Term of endearment for infants. Also used condescendingly toward anyone who doesn't know how to use a stove.
"Smustle."
— A specific dance. The Sim will perform it without warning when sufficiently happy. The party has stopped asking why.
"Vadish!"
— Expression of excitement or triumph. Heard loudly upon defeating an enemy, finding a rare collectible, or finishing a particularly good meal.
"Woohoo."
— An intimate act between consenting adult Sims. The only Simlish word universally understood across every known plane. Scholars of linguistics are unsettled by this.

Party Integration

The Sim in the Adventuring Party

The Sim is a wild card by design. Their racial features introduce small chaos into otherwise serious situations. They are also genuinely useful, emotionally loyal, and deeply confused by a world without need bars.

Social Dynamics

The Social need forces the Sim to engage with the party constantly. The party will know the Sim better than the Sim knows themselves within three days of travel. The Sim's Mood Sight means they know exactly what every party member is feeling at all times. They are usually polite about this. Usually. There will be a session where they are not polite about it and the party will remember it for a long time.

Combat Role

Depends on Career translation. Their Plumbob broadcasts emotional state to enemies — a Tense Sim is easier to target with concentration-disruption tactics. A tactically savvy DM will weaponize this. A tactically savvy player will learn to stay Fine. The goal of staying Fine will, itself, produce Tense.

Final Note on the Plumbob

No god, wizard, archmage, or cosmic entity encountered in Faerûn has been able to explain the Plumbob. Mystra has refused to comment. Azuth referred further questions to the Simic Combine, who are in a different setting entirely. Tyr has ruled it outside his purview. The Plumbob continues floating above the Sim's head regardless.
