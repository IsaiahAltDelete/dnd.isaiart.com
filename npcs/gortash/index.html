Origins & History

The Making of a Tyrant

🔗

Sold by His Own Blood

Born Enver Flymm to cobblers Dravo and Sally in the Lower City, he was sold as a boy to pay off his parents' debts — traded to a warlock who delivered him straight to Raphael. He grew up in the House of Hope, tortured for sport by the jailer Nubaldin. The cruelty didn't break him. It built him.

⚔️

Arms Dealer to Archduke

After escaping the Hells, Gortash muscled his way into Baldur's Gate's black market — infernal iron, exotic weapons, Chionthar Valley supply chains. He leveraged military knowledge to embed himself with the patriars, becoming a "strategic advisor" before any of them realized they were already his pawns.

⚙️

The Steel Watch

His magnum opus: a clockwork army of constructs powered by infernal engines — the same technology implanted in Karlach's chest via a deal with Zariel. He used Karlach as a test subject, then sold her to Avernus. The Steel Watch "protected" Baldur's Gate. In reality, it enslaved it.

👁️

Chosen of Bane

Gortash is the Chosen of Bane, the Black Lord of Tyranny. He and the Dark Urge orchestrated the enslavement of an elder brain — the Absolute — using the stolen Crown of Karsus. His god of Fear, Hate, and Power speaks through his corpse long after death. Even dead, Gortash serves Bane's ends.

⚙ D&D Multiclass Analysis ⚙

Class & Subclass

The Multiclass Build

Level 10 · Primary Artificer Subclass: Armorer

Gortash is, at his core, an engineer. He didn't just deal weapons — he built them, perfected them, and turned infernal metallurgy into a political weapon. The Steel Watch is a D&D construct army, full stop.

  • Infuse Item — his crossbow "Fabricated Arbalest" is a classic infused weapon
  • Steel Defender — prototype Steel Watch unit = Artificer companion
  • Armor Model (Guardian) — his gauntlet, high-collar suit = integrated armor
  • Replicate Magic Item — infernal engine tech via Zariel's deal
  • Flash of Genius — tactical genius reaction in INT-based fights
Class Investment 10 / 20 Levels
Level 6 · Secondary Warlock Subclass: The Fiend

Raised in the literal House of Hope by a devil. Chosen of a god whose divine favor functions exactly like a Warlock patron — power through servitude, influence through dark covenant. He didn't choose Bane. He was selected.

  • Dark One's Blessing — Bane's power surges through him in combat
  • Eldritch Blast + Repelling Blast — his thunder-resistant aura maps to this
  • Pact of the Blade — Fabricated Arbalest as pact weapon (ranged)
  • Agonizing Blast — Authority advantage on Intimidation is this, mechanically
  • Fiendish Resilience — surviving infernal politics, Raphael's dungeon
Class Investment 6 / 20 Levels
Level 4 · Tertiary Rogue Subclass: Mastermind

Before he was an archduke he was a smuggler. A black-market arms dealer who moved product through back channels and bought loyalty with leverage. The Mastermind doesn't fight — it orchestrates.

  • Master of Intrigue — disguises, forgeries, the political theater of his coronation
  • Master of Tactics — Help as a Bonus Action; uses allies as tactical pieces
  • Sneak Attack — Fabricated Arbalest catching enemies off-guard
  • Expertise (Deception, Insight) — reading and lying to everyone, always
  • Uncanny Dodge — the politically teflon nature of his rise to power
Class Investment 4 / 20 Levels

Combat & Roleplay Features

What He Brings to the Table

👁️
Authority (Bane's Blessing)

Advantage on all Intimidation and Insight checks. This isn't charisma — it's divine fear. People don't respect Gortash. They're just too afraid not to agree with him.

⚡
Invoke the Black Hand

Phase 2 transformation — Avatar of Tyranny. Temporary HP surge, Chains of Tyranny (8d8 force damage), summoning Manifestations of Tyranny. The god steps in when the man isn't enough.

🏹
Fabricated Arbalest

A custom-engineered crossbow that fires lightning bolts. Artificer through and through — not just a weapon, but a prototype. The man brought a railgun to a sword fight.

🔧
Steel Watch (Construct Army)

The ultimate Artificer capstone played out in real-world politics. Fifty-foot clockwork golems powered by infernal engines, controlling the entire city under the guise of "protection." Artificer Steel Defender at municipal scale.

🎭
Master of Intrigue (Mastermind)

Reinvented himself from a cobbler's son to an archduke. Used forgery, disguise, and social infiltration to ascend through Baldur's Gate's patrician class without them ever realizing they'd let the wolf inside.

🕯️
Dark One's Own Luck (Fiend)

Survived Raphael's dungeons. Survived the black market. Survived Zariel's deal. Survived the rise of the Absolute. Gortash doesn't just succeed — he has a disturbing pattern of not dying when he should.

Phase One · Active Abilities

Actions & Attacks

🏹
Ranged Attack
Action 18m Range

Standard ranged attack with the Fabricated Arbalest. His DEX modifier (+5) and +2 weapon enchantment make this accurate as hell — and he has Crossbow Expert so you can't bait him into melee Disadvantage.

1d10 + 7 Piercing · 8–17 dmg
☀️
Dazzling Ray
Action Concentration Short Rest

A beam of brilliant radiant light that blinds every creature in its path. Mechanically a reskinned Sunbeam — DEX save or be blinded (Disadvantage on attacks, attack range shrinks to 3m, enemies gain Advantage against you). While concentrating, Gortash can recast it — though doing so may burn him for 1d4 Fire. He opens almost every fight with this.

2d10 Radiant · 2–20 dmg · DEX Save
✨
Illuminating Shot
Bonus Action No Cooldown

Fires a shimmering bolt that inflicts Radiating Orb — a stacking debuff that reduces the target's Attack Roll by −1 per turn and illuminates them (no hiding). No cooldown means he can use it every turn alongside his main action. Pairs disgustingly well with Dazzling Ray.

1d4 Piercing + Radiating Orb (−1 ATK/turn)
💣
Mechanical Throw
Bonus Action 18m Range

Throw as a Bonus Action — using Smokepowder Bombs for 3d4+9 Force damage in a 4-meter radius. The man keeps grenades on him as a politician. Of course he does. He'll use these on clustered party members and laugh about it.

3d4 + 9 Force · 4m radius AoE
🛡 Passive Features 🛡

Always Active · Cannot Be Removed

Passive Abilities

Authority

Advantage on Intimidation and Insight checks. Bane's blessing makes people fear and read him simultaneously.

Crossbow Expert: Point-Blank

Crossbow attacks in melee range don't receive Disadvantage. Pulling him close doesn't help you — he just shoots you in the face.

Dark Devotion

Advantage on saving throws against Charmed and Frightened. You can't charm the man who literally controls the city.

Dauntless

Completely immune to Frightened and all emotion-altering conditions. Bane's Chosen doesn't do fear. That's someone else's department.

Enervating Suffusion

Unarmed attacks deal +1d4 Force damage. From his Netherstone-Studded Gauntlet — infernal iron fused with Netherese artifact energy.

High Spellcasting

+1 to Spell Save DC (total DC 18 on Balanced, 20 on Tactician). From the gauntlet. His spells are harder to resist than they should be.

Magic Resistance

Advantage on all saving throws against spells and magical effects. Raphael's dungeon either taught him how to resist magic or he just absorbed it from proximity to devils.

Permanently Armed

Cannot be disarmed. The Fabricated Arbalest stays in his hands no matter what. It's not just a weapon — it's a part of him.

Scintillating

+1 to Charisma ability checks and saving throws. From the Tyrannical Jackboots — even his footwear enhances his social dominance.

Shell of Resistance

Complete Thunder immunity + elemental resistance — active while Reflectoguards and the Micromodron Force Curtains are intact. Destroy them to strip this off.

Opportunity Attack

Attacks enemies that move out of melee reach. With his DEX score of 20 and a +2 crossbow, fleeing him is a terrible idea.

Reflectoguard

A protective field on nearby allies that bounces thrown items and arrows back at attackers. Spells pass through; projectiles don't. A battlefield-control passive that punishes ranged parties.

☠ Phase 2 · Avatar of Tyranny ☠

Triggered at 75% HP · Once Per Combat

The Black Lord Arrives

👁️
Invoke the Black Hand
Reaction Phase 2 Trigger Once Per Combat

Automatically fires when Gortash drops to 75% HP. He calls upon Bane's tyrannical aid, gaining 150 temporary hit points and transforming into the Avatar of Tyranny. Multiple Manifestations of Tyranny spawn across the room, each one adding a stacking Bane's Brutality aura (+1 STR) to Gortash and nearby Black Gauntlet allies. One Bane's Fist entity is also inserted at the end of the turn order. The god steps in because the man is losing.

⛓️
Tyrant's Bindings
Action Concentration Phase 2 Only

Chains all party members in range to Gortash via force chains. Targets that fail the save are bound. When the chains break on his next turn, they explode outward — dealing 8d8 Force damage. The only counter is breaking his concentration via hits or an Arrow of Arcane Interference. If this goes off uncontested your whole party is eating a bus.

8d8 Force · 8–64 dmg on chain break · CON Save
✊
Bane's Fist
Summon Phase 2 Only

Conjures the divine aspect of Bane — a colossal spectral fist that hangs suspended in the air for a round before crashing down. At the end of each of its turns it punches the ground beneath it for devastating Force damage in an area. Priority one: get the hell out from under it.

12d10 Force · 12–120 dmg · AoE crush
🖤
Hand of Shadow
Summon Phase 2 Only

A massive black shadowed hand materializes above the party. It forms a fist and slams down repeatedly — targeting the area below it. Unlike Bane's Fist, this one tracks. Leave its radius or it will grind your party to paste over multiple turns.

👿
Manifestations of Tyranny
Passive Aura Phase 2 Only

Spectral aspects of Bane that spawn in fixed positions around the room. Each one within range applies a stack of Bane's Brutality to Gortash and nearby Black Gauntlet allies (+1 STR, can stack). If Gortash stands inside multiple overlapping auras this gets out of hand fast. Kill them — or keep him away from them.

✦ Final Verdict ✦

Lord Enver Gortash is a 20th-level Artificer (Armorer) 10 / Warlock (The Fiend) 6 / Rogue (Mastermind) 4. He is the rare villain who wins not through raw power but through infrastructure. Every class tier reflects a different phase of his life — the tortured child who learned to build as a survival mechanism, the pact-bound chosen who traded his soul for divine authority, and the smuggler who learned that the sharpest weapon is knowing exactly what everyone else wants and making them think you can give it to them. Gortash doesn't conquer cities. He engineers them to conquer themselves.

— Character Analysis · Baldur's Gate III · Act III
