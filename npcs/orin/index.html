Origins & History

The Making of a Monster

🩸

Bhaalspawn Royalty

Orin is the granddaughter of Sarevok Anchev — himself a near-god of murder — making her pure Bhaalspawn twice over. She grew up steeped in blood ritual, assassination, and worship. Her connection to Bhaal isn't metaphorical. The murder god literally flows through her veins, granting her the Slayer transformation others only dream of.

🎭

The Shapeshifter

A Changeling by race, Orin can perfectly mimic any humanoid she has observed. She uses this not just to infiltrate — she uses it to psychologically destroy her targets. She kidnaps a party member, replaces them flawlessly for days, then forces you to watch. She doesn't just kill. She erases.

👁️

Bhaal's Favoured

Of the three Chosen, Orin is the only one Bhaal actually gives a damn about on a personal level — not strategically, but because she IS him in mortal form. She craves his approval with the same desperate hunger she channels into murder. Her sadism isn't random. It's devotional art offered to her god.

⚔️

The Dark Urge's Rival

Orin's core motivation is dominance over the Dark Urge — her kin and competition for Bhaal's favour. She orchestrated the Urge's fall from grace to claim primacy. Her hostage gambit, her kidnapping mechanic, her entire challenge structure — it's all one long kill brag directed at a single rival.

🗡 D&D Multiclass Analysis 🗡

Class & Subclass

The Multiclass Build

Level 10 · Primary Rogue Subclass: Arcane Trickster

Larian confirmed Orin's combat design was modeled after the Arcane Trickster. She's a shortsword specialist who blends illusion magic, stealth, and deception — the epitome of a Rogue who uses spells not to blast but to confuse, misdirect, and vanish.

  • Sneak Attack — her bleed attacks exploit Advantage relentlessly
  • Uncanny Dodge — reaction to halve incoming damage
  • Reliable Talent — skill checks floor of 10 on proficient skills
  • Mage Hand Legerdemain — telekinetic manipulation
  • Illusion/Enchantment spells — Phantasmal Force, Suggestion
Class Investment10 / 20 Levels
Level 6 · Secondary Barbarian Subclass: Berserker

The Slayer form IS Rage. Pure, divine, Bhaal-fuelled Berserker rage that pushes her stats off the charts and transforms her from a finesse killer into a juggernaut. Berserker specifically because Frenzy gives her extra bonus action attacks — the same relentless assault the Slayer dishes out.

  • Rage — damage resistance, bonus damage, can't be charmed
  • Reckless Attack — Advantage on attacks for Sneak Attack setup
  • Frenzy — bonus action melee attack every turn while raging
  • Fast Movement — extra speed in the Slayer form's chasing game
  • Mindless Rage — the Slayer does not stop, period
Class Investment6 / 20 Levels
Level 4 · Tertiary Cleric Subclass: Death Domain

She is a priestess of Bhaal. Functionally, mechanically, lore-literally. Death Domain gives her Reaper (necrotic spells hit multiple targets), Undead-like Undying quality, and Cause Fear — all of which map onto her actual abilities. Her cultists use Bhaal's Edict and animate blood. That's Death Cleric territory.

  • Reaper — necrotic cantrips target two creatures
  • Cause Fear — her terror aura on the cultist altar
  • Animate Dead — the Sanguine Animation / Bloodmarrow Skeletons
  • Channel Divinity: Touch of Death — necrotic bonus damage
  • Divine Strike — necrotic damage on weapon attacks
Class Investment4 / 20 Levels

🗡 Weapon Actions & Abilities 🗡

Phase One · Active Abilities

Actions & Attacks

🗡️
Dual Shortsword Attack
ActionExtra Attack ×2

Three attacks per turn with her twin shortswords — Crimson Mischief and Bloodthirst — dealing piercing and necrotic damage. Each hit procs her Bhaal bleed effects and stacks toward Sneak Attack. She hits faster than any other Chosen.

1d6+5 Piercing + Necrotic per hit · ×3
🩸
Bhaalspawn Bleed
On HitStacking

Every melee hit applies a Bleed condition — targets take ongoing necrotic damage each turn they're bleeding. This isn't just damage — Bleed stacks let her proc additional effects and set up Sneak Attack advantage. Let her hit you twice and you're already hemorrhaging.

1d4 Necrotic per turn · stacking Bleed
🎭
Let the Slaughter Begin
ActionTactical Threat

Orin marks all cultists and allies in the arena. If any marked target is killed — by you or by her — she absorbs the death and gains a stack of AC permanently. Kill her cultists and she becomes harder to hit. Leave them alive and they'll chant to restore her Unstoppable. There is no good option.

🩻
Sanguine Animation
ReactionBlood Necromancy

Orin animates blood pools and corpses nearby into Bloodmarrow Skeletons under her control. Knocking enemies off the altar platform reduces available corpses. A Bhaal-specific Death Cleric trick — blood on the floor isn't wasted, it's recruited.

🛡 Passive Features 🛡

Always Active

Passive Abilities

Unstoppable (7 Stacks)

Every attack against her deals only 1 damage until all 7 stacks are consumed. Must be broken by hitting her 7+ times before real damage lands. The most mechanically frustrating passive in the game.

Magic Resistance

Advantage on all saving throws against spells. Your Control spells are substantially less reliable. She's not a caster — she just won't be stopped by one either.

Alert (Cannot Be Surprised)

Cannot be caught off-guard. No surprise round. You will never get the drop on a woman who has been hunting you across three acts.

Uncanny Dodge

Reaction to reduce incoming damage by half once per round. A Rogue's bread-and-butter survival tool. Even when you land a big hit, she halves it.

Reliable Talent

Skill checks she's proficient in always roll minimum 10. Her Deception, Stealth, and Performance checks are effectively un-botchable.

Extra Attack ×2

Three attacks per action in her humanoid form. Combined with her bonus action off-hand attack, she hits 4+ times per round. Every hit adds Bleed. The math gets scary fast.

Shapeshifting (Changeling)

Perfect mimicry of any humanoid. Socially, she is literally untrackable. In combat she used it to get close for kills — disguised as a merchant, a guard, your companion.

Bhaal's Edict (from Bhaal)

In Honour Mode, Bhaal acts at the end of every round — targeting a random party member with an edict to deal the killing blow to someone or die. Orin herself doesn't cast this. Her god does. Automatically.

Fey Ancestry

Advantage on saves vs Charmed; can't be magically put to sleep. Her Changeling heritage makes her mind slippery. You cannot charm or sleep the murder god's blade.

🩸 Phase 2 · The Slayer 🩸

Bhaalspawn Transformation · Combat Trigger

Bhaal Made Flesh

🩸
Slayer Transformation
Phase 2 TriggerBhaalspawn Form

When pressured, Orin abandons her humanoid form and transforms into the Slayer — Bhaal's apex predator in mortal flesh. Her stats surge: she becomes massively more powerful, gains new movement abilities including Relentless Lunge, and continues applying Bleed on every hit. The Unstoppable stacks can only be refreshed by her cultists' ritual chanting — kill them to lock her out. If the Dark Urge also has the Slayer form, this becomes a literal mirror fight.

💨
Relentless Lunge
ActionSlayer Only

Orin launches into the air and crashes down in a radius, knocking down everyone caught in the AoE. This is her primary repositioning and crowd-control tool in Slayer form — she can leap across the entire altar platform and land on clustered party members. Spread your party or get knocked flat.

AoE Slam · Knockdown · Positioning gap-closer
🧟
Unstoppable Refresh (via Reapers)
ConditionalRitual Mechanic

Cultists on the opposite end of the altar are chanting a ritual that continuously refreshes Orin's Unstoppable stacks. They're protected by Sanctuary and can only be harmed by AoE spells. Eliminate them and Orin loses her ability to regenerate the barrier — every remaining Unstoppable stack can then be stripped away conventionally.

💀
Slayer Multiattack
ActionSlayer Only

In Slayer form, Orin attacks multiple times with bestial slashes that inflict Bleed on every hit. The damage output is higher than Phase 1, the hits are harder to avoid, and every Bleed stack is counting toward her Sanguine Animation fuel. She's not just killing you — she's preparing the blood pool for the next wave of skeletons.

Multiple slashing hits · Bleed on each · escalating Necrotic DoT

✦ Final Verdict ✦

Orin the Red is a 20th-level Rogue (Arcane Trickster) 10 / Barbarian (Berserker) 6 / Cleric (Death Domain) 4. She is the purest embodiment of what happens when a divine mandate to murder is paired with supernatural social stealth and the raw physical power of Bhaalspawn heritage. Where Gortash engineers and Ketheric grinds, Orin hunts. The Unstoppable mechanic forces you to earn every point of damage against her. The cultist ritual mechanic makes you choose between eliminating her support and hardening her AC. Her Slayer form strips away the theatrical pretence of the shapeshifter and delivers Bhaal himself in her skin. She is the most chaotic, the most personal, and mechanically the most relentless of the three Chosen — because murder gods don't stop.
