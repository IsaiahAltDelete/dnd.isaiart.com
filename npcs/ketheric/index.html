Origins & History

A Century of Grief

⚔️

Paladin of Selûne — Then Shar

Ketheric Thorm was once a devoted Selûnite paladin — a husband, father, governor of Reithwin Town. When his wife died, grief cracked him. When Isobel was killed, it shattered him completely. He turned to Shar — goddess of loss — seeking oblivion. She gave him exactly that: the gift of forgetting the pain, at the cost of his soul.

💀

The Shadow Curse

Defeated a century ago by a coalition of Harpers and Emerald Grove druids, Ketheric's death rattle unleashed the Shadow Curse — a necrotic blight that consumed Reithwin Town and the surrounding lands. Everything within died or twisted. He was already shaping death on a regional scale before Myrkul ever touched him.

🌙

Myrkul's Bargain

Myrkul, Lord of Bones, offered Ketheric what no other god could: true immortality through the imprisonment of Aylin, the Nightsong, a godling daughter of Selûne. As long as Aylin remained trapped in his soul cage, Ketheric could not die. He fed on her divine life force for a century. The god of death made him unkillable with someone else's immortality.

👧

Isobel — The Price of Everything

Myrkul resurrected Isobel as payment for Ketheric's service. But when she saw what her father had become — the broken, undead warlord looming over a cursed wasteland — she fled. Protected by Selûne, she became a cleric of the Moonmaiden. Ketheric spent a century building an apocalypse army to bring her home, and she still wouldn't come. He is a tragedy as much as a villain.

⚔ D&D Multiclass Analysis ⚔

Class & Subclass

The Multiclass Build

Level 10 · Primary Paladin Subclass: Oathbreaker

Ketheric is a paladin who broke his oath to Selûne and converted first to Shar, then to Myrkul. The Oathbreaker subclass was designed for exactly this — divine power warped into necrotic tyranny. His AC 22, Divine Smite, Extra Attack, and Aura abilities are all straight paladin. The flair on the wiki lists "Improved Divine Smite" specifically.

  • Improved Divine Smite — automatic necrotic damage on every hit
  • Aura of Hate — nearby fiends and undead gain CHA bonus to damage
  • Aura of Protection — save bonus equal to CHA modifier
  • Animate Dead (Oathbreaker) — his necromite spam is this
  • Extra Attack — triple action economy in melee
Class Investment10 / 20 Levels
Level 6 · Secondary Wizard Subclass: School of Necromancy

The necromantic army — the Shadow Curse, the Necromites, Incubate Death, Finger of Death — all require a Necromancy Wizard spine. He has been a military general for hundreds of years, and a century of undeath under Myrkul would absolutely develop deep necromantic spellcasting. The Apostle form is Necromancy pushed past the edge of mortality.

  • Grim Harvest — regains HP when creatures he controls die nearby
  • Undead Thralls — animated dead have bonus HP and attack
  • Finger of Death — his Apostle form's execute spell
  • Incubate Death — spawning skeletal Necromites
  • Inured to Undeath — necrotic resistance, max HP raise cap
Class Investment6 / 20 Levels
Level 4 · Tertiary Fighter Subclass: Battle Master

Ketheric has been a military general for hundreds of years. He doesn't just swing a warhammer — he commands the field. Battle Master gives him Superiority Dice for tactical maneuvers: Menacing Attack (Frightened), Trip Attack (Knockdown), Commander's Strike. His military intelligence and buff to unit targeting directly mirror the Battle Master toolkit.

  • Action Surge — an extra action of attacks mid-boss-fight
  • Menacing Attack — Frightened condition from warhammer blows
  • Trip Attack — the knockdown potential in Phase 1
  • Tactical Report — commanding minions to focus targets
  • Second Wind — minor self-heal in prolonged fights
Class Investment4 / 20 Levels

⚔ Weapons & Abilities ⚔

Phase One · Active Abilities

Actions & Attacks

🔨
Warhammer Strike
ActionExtra Attack

Ketheric's primary melee attack — a devastating warhammer blow that inflicts Improved Divine Smite necrotic damage on every hit automatically. His STR 20 means this hits hard before any smite bonus. Disarming him forces him to use a Flail of Myrkul instead, shutting off his smite economy.

1d10+5 Bludgeoning + 1d4 Psychic (warhammer) + necrotic smite
💀
Incubate Death
ActionNecromancy

Spawns a skeletal involucre. After 1 turn, the involucre explodes for 3d8 Necrotic damage and births a living Necromite that serves Ketheric. In Phase 2, up to four Necromites spawn at the arena edges every round. Managing these is the primary mechanical challenge of the entire Act 2 climax.

3d8 Necrotic (explosion) · Spawns Necromite minion
⚡
Tactical Report
Bonus ActionCommander

Commands nearby undead and cultist troops to prioritise a specific enemy target. Allied units are substantially more likely to focus that creature. A Battle Master ability played out at warlord scale — he doesn't fight alone, he coordinates an army.

😨
Death Knell Aura
PassiveFear / Necrotic

A passive aura that numbs all nearby creatures — dealing necrotic damage and potentially Frightening them on failed saves. His Aura of Hate simultaneously boosts nearby fiend and undead damage by his CHA modifier (+4). The man is a walking debuff field and buff beacon simultaneously.

Necrotic aura · Frightened on fail · +4 to ally damage
🔮
Spectral Hammer (Phase 1)
ActionRanged Necrotic

Launches a hammer of spectral force at a creature his minions are attacking. A ranged follow-up that punishes enemies who draw his undead army's attention. Death comes from every direction in his encounter — melee from him, spectral force from range, necrotic aura from proximity.

Force/Necrotic damage · Targets enemies engaged with minions
🛡️
Immortality (via Aylin)
PermanentPhase 1 Lock

As long as the Nightsong (Aylin) remains imprisoned in the soul cage, Ketheric is absolutely immune to all damage in Phase 2. No cheesing, no bypassing — you must use the Help action on Aylin to free her first. His immortality is literally a hostage situation. Break the cage, break the man.

🛡 Passive Features 🛡

Always Active

Passive Abilities

Divine Health

The divine magic within him prevents disease. An undead paladin immune to disease. His body is beyond mortal infection — it's already dead.

Improved Divine Smite

Every single weapon attack automatically deals additional necrotic damage. No spell slots required. No decision. Every hit is a smite, always.

Fey Ancestry

Half-Elf heritage. Advantage on saves vs Charmed, magic can't put him to sleep. Even his racial traits tilt toward "cannot be controlled."

Aura of Hate

Nearby fiends and undead add his Charisma modifier (+4) to their weapon damage rolls. Every Necromite and undead ally is hitting harder just because he's standing there.

Aura of Protection

Adds his CHA modifier (+4) to all saving throws for himself and nearby allies. His presence makes everyone harder to control or kill.

Darkvision (12m)

Sees perfectly in the dark. He controls the Shadow Curse — a literal darkness that killed everything around Reithwin for a century. Of course he sees in the dark.

Defence (+1 AC in armour)

His Reaper's Embrace armour + Defence feat = the 22 AC that makes Phase 1 Ketheric feel like hitting a fortified wall. His total AC is among the highest of any non-construct boss.

Extra Attack

Multiple attacks per action. Combined with Improved Divine Smite on each, his action economy in melee is terrifying for a creature who also commands an undead army.

Immutable Form (Apostle)

The Apostle of Myrkul cannot be polymorphed or otherwise altered in form. The god does not allow his vessel to be changed by mortal magic.

💀 Phase 2 · Apostle of Myrkul 💀

Final Transformation · Myrkul Takes the Wheel

The God of Death Arrives

💀
Apostle of Myrkul Transformation
Phase 2 TriggerDivine Avatar

When Ketheric's mortal form is destroyed, he pleads to Myrkul — and the god answers. Ketheric casts himself into a brine pool and emerges as the Apostle of Myrkul: a colossal, stationary skeletal avatar of divine death. He gains 225 HP, a new AC of 18, and a completely different attack set. He does not move — 0 movement speed — but doesn't need to. The arena comes to him. His Bone Chill aura emanates from the platform and prevents all healing. His Necromite spawn rate doubles. And he can absorb a Necromite as a reaction to charge Finger of Death — an instant death spell that kills outright on failed save.

💀
Myrkul's Presence (Bone Chill)
Permanent AuraApostle Only

The Apostle exudes the god of death's unholy will, Bone Chilling all nearby creatures. Bone Chill prevents all healing — spells, potions, Second Wind, everything. To heal party members, you must exit the circular platform, lose the condition, heal, then re-enter. You cannot revive downed allies within his aura. He doesn't need to kill you — he just needs to outlast your resources.

Bone Chill: Blocks all healing · Necrotic damage on proximity
☠️
Finger of Death
ActionVia Consume the FaithfulApostle Only

A Level 7 necromancy spell. The target must make a Constitution save or take 7d8+30 necrotic damage — and if they die, they rise as a zombie permanently under Myrkul's control. The Apostle can only cast this by consuming a Necromite as a reaction. Kill every Necromite before it reaches him and this ability is locked out entirely. Let one through and someone in your party might die permanently.

7d8 + 30 Necrotic · CON Save or die · victim rises as zombie
💀
Gaze of the Dead / Reaper's Scythe
ActionApostle Only

His two-attack pattern: Reaper's Scythe in melee (massive bludgeoning damage, AoE slam) and Gaze of the Dead as a ranged attack that fires necrotic beams at distant targets. If no one is in melee range, he substitutes Call of the Damned — which hits everyone for 10–37 damage. You must keep someone adjacent to him at all times or face a party-wide nuke.

Ranged: necrotic beam · Melee: scythe slam · No target: 10–37 AoE
🧟
Consume the Faithful (Reaction)
ReactionApostle Only

When a Necromite reaches the Apostle, it can use a reaction to consume it — absorbing the undead creature's essence and gaining Reaper's Reward, which charges Finger of Death. This reaction fires before your turn, meaning you cannot kill a Necromite on your turn if it's already adjacent to him at round's end. You must manage the arena perimeter.

🦴
Necromite Wave
Each Round EndSummon

At the end of every round in Phase 2, up to four new Necromites spawn at random positions on the arena edges. They then walk toward the Apostle. They're resistant to piercing damage but vulnerable to bludgeoning and radiant. If the Nightsong (Aylin) has been freed, she intercepts them. If she hasn't — you're dealing with four fresh necromites every single turn while maintaining melee range and healing rotation.

✦ Final Verdict ✦

General Ketheric Thorm is a 20th-level Paladin (Oathbreaker) 10 / Wizard (School of Necromancy) 6 / Fighter (Battle Master) 4. He is the only one of the three Chosen who fights as a juggernaut rather than a tactician or an assassin. His build reflects his history perfectly: a paladin who broke his oath and let divine corruption transmute his devotion into necrotic power, a necromancer who has spent a century studying death from the inside, and a general who commands armies rather than fighting alone. The Apostle of Myrkul phase is the most mechanically layered boss fight in Act 2 — not because Ketheric is clever, but because the god of death doesn't let people stop dying. He ends the act with his daughter's name on his lips, which is the most damning thing Larian could have done to him.
